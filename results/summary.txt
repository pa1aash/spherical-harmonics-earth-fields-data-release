NOTES (see AUDIT_REPORT.md):
  * Fair comparison: each field's eps is measured against ITS OWN reference degree (gravity 360, magnetic main field 13). The
    decay exponent (log-log slope) is the scale-free comparator. The IGRF main field decays fastest because it is a deep-core,
    dipole-dominated signal that is intrinsically band-limited to l<=13; the geoid is intermediate; the CRUSTAL magnetic field
    decays SLOWER than gravity. So 'magnetic compresses better' is true ONLY for the core/main field, not magnetism in general.
  * Gravity threshold sensitivity: the geoid's low-degree power (esp. degree 2) depends on the reference-ellipsoid/tide-system
    treatment; dropping degree 2 raises the 1%% threshold from ~191 to ~219. The decay exponent and the qualitative gap are
    robust to this choice. Magnetic results are verified against the raw Gauss coefficients to ~1e-13 (no such sensitivity).
